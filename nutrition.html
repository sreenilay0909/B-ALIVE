Nutrition Overview

1. Macronutrients

Macronutrients are the nutrients that provide us with energy and are necessary for growth and maintenance.

  • Carbohydrates:
    • Primary source of energy for the body.
    • Types include simple carbs (sugars) and complex carbs (starches and fiber).
    • Found in fruits, vegetables, grains, and legumes.
  • Proteins:
    • Essential for growth, repair, and maintenance of body tissues.
    • Made up of amino acids, some of which are essential (must be obtained from food).
    • Sources include meat, fish, dairy, beans, and nuts.
  • Fats:
    • Important for energy, hormone production, and nutrient absorption.
    • Types include saturated, unsaturated, and trans fats.
    • Healthy sources include avocados, olive oil, nuts, and fatty fish.

2. Micronutrients

Micronutrients are vital for various biochemical functions in the body.

  • Vitamins:
    • Organic compounds needed in small quantities.
    • Examples include vitamin C (important for immune function) and vitamin D (crucial for bone health).
  • Minerals:
    • Inorganic elements that play critical roles in health.
    • Support processes like bone health (calcium) and oxygen transport (iron).

3. Water

Water is essential for life. It plays key roles in:

  • Hydration
  • Digestion
  • Regulating body temperature

It's crucial to drink enough fluids daily.

4. Dietary Fiber

Fiber is important for digestive health and can help maintain a healthy weight.

  • Found in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes.
  • Helps regulate blood sugar levels and lowers cholesterol.

5. Balanced Diet

A balanced diet includes a variety of foods from all food groups in appropriate proportions. It is essential for:

  • Providing essential nutrients
  • Maintaining a healthy weight
  • Reducing the risk of chronic diseases

6. Nutritional Needs

Nutritional needs vary based on:

  • Age
  • Gender
  • Activity level
  • Health status

It's important to tailor your diet to meet your individual needs.

7. Food Labels

Understanding nutrition labels helps you make healthier food choices by providing information on:

  • Calories
  • Macronutrients
  • Micronutrients
